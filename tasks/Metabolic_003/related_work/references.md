# Related Work: NAFLD Prevalence and Risk Factors

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Prevalence and factors associated with NAFLD detected by vibration controlled transient elastography among US adults: Results from NHANES 2017-2018
- **Authors**: Zhang X, Heredia NI, Balakrishnan M, Thrift AP
- **Journal**: PLOS ONE
- **Year**: 2021
- **DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pone.0252164
- **PMID**: 34081733
- **Relevance**: Uses direct measurement (transient elastography, CAP ≥248 dB/m) rather than an index to define NAFLD in NHANES 2017-2018 (n=4,024). Found much higher prevalence (56.7% by CAP vs 12.4% by elevated liver enzymes), highlighting the critical impact of NAFLD definition method on prevalence estimates. Multivariable logistic regression identified obesity, metabolic syndrome, diabetes, and inadequate physical activity as independent risk factors.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Prevalence of High and Moderate Risk Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease Among Adults in the United States, 1999-2016
- **Authors**: Golabi P, Paik JM, AlQahtani S, Younossi Y, Staiculescu D, Younossi ZM
- **Journal**: Clinical Gastroenterology and Hepatology
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.cgh.2021.12.015
- **PMID**: 34929391
- **Relevance**: Comprehensive NHANES analysis spanning 1999-2016 from the Younossi group (leading NAFLD epidemiology team). Published in high-impact CGH (IF~12). Provides long-term trend context and risk stratification using composite endpoints including NASH risk scores. Establishes temporal trajectory against which 2011-2018 estimates can be compared.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Prevalence and risk factors of nonalcoholic fatty liver disease, high-risk nonalcoholic steatohepatitis, and fibrosis among lean United States adults: NHANES 2017-2020
- **Authors**: Kalligeros M, Vassilopoulos A, Vassilopoulos S, et al.
- **Journal**: World Journal of Hepatology
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.4254/wjh.v15.i11.1177
- **PMID**: 38023978
- **Relevance**: Focuses on the lean NAFLD phenotype (BMI <25) in NHANES 2017-2020, demonstrating that NAFLD occurs in non-obese individuals with distinct risk factor profiles. Uses VCTE-based CAP and LSM for NAFLD and fibrosis assessment. Complements the index-based approach of the target study by providing imaging-validated estimates in the lean subpopulation.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Prevalence of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease and risk factors for advanced fibrosis and mortality in the United States
- **Authors**: Le MH, Devaki P, Ha NB, et al.
- **Journal**: PLOS ONE
- **Year**: 2017
- **DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pone.0173499
- **PMID**: 28346543
- **Relevance**: Earlier NHANES-based NAFLD prevalence study using NHANES III (with liver ultrasound) and NHANES 2003-2014 (with noninvasive indices). Provides historical baseline prevalence estimates and mortality outcomes, establishing the epidemiologic trajectory that the target study updates.
