## Abstract Summary
This study estimated the prevalence of NAFLD and significant NAFLD fibrosis in U.S. adults using NHANES 2011-2018 data. NAFLD was defined as US Fatty Liver Index (USFLI) ≥30 in the absence of other causes of liver disease (excessive alcohol intake, chronic hepatitis B, chronic hepatitis C). Significant fibrosis was defined using NAFLD fibrosis score (NFS ≥0.676) and fibrosis-4 index (FIB-4 ≥2.67). Among 7,429 adults aged ≥20 years, the weighted national prevalence of NAFLD was 26.7% (95% CI: 25.3%-28.1%). Prevalence was higher among those aged ≥65 years, males, Mexican Americans, those with BMI ≥35 kg/m² (class 2-3 obesity), and those with type 2 diabetes. Among NAFLD-positive individuals, 18.1% had a high probability of advanced fibrosis by NFS and 3.7% by FIB-4.

## Key Methods
- Cross-sectional analysis of NHANES 2011-2018 (4 cycles)
- NAFLD defined by USFLI ≥30 with exclusion of viral hepatitis and excessive alcohol
- Advanced fibrosis assessed by NFS and FIB-4 with established cutoffs
- Survey-weighted prevalence estimation
- Subgroup analyses by age, sex, race/ethnicity, BMI, diabetes status

## Key Findings
1. Weighted national NAFLD prevalence: 26.7% (95% CI: 25.3%-28.1%)
2. NAFLD prevalence by CAP (2017-2018 subsample): ~56.7% with lower CAP threshold
3. Advanced fibrosis among NAFLD: 18.1% by NFS, 3.7% by FIB-4
4. Higher prevalence in males, Mexican Americans, those with obesity (BMI ≥35) and type 2 diabetes
5. Among NAFLD-positive: 67.9% male, 25.8% aged ≥65, 74.1% obese (BMI ≥30)
6. National projected population estimate: 84.3 million adults with complete data

## Dataset Used
NHANES 2011-2012, 2013-2014, 2015-2016, 2017-2018 cycles
