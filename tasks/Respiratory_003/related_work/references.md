# Related Work: Insulin Resistance and Obstructive Sleep Apnea

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Evaluate the Relationship Between Obstructive Sleep Apnea and Metabolic Syndrome in Real-World Data
- **Authors**: Tang R, Li C, Di D, et al.
- **Journal**: Nature and Science of Sleep
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.2147/NSS.S433514
- **PMID**: 38445023
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2005-2008 study (n=11,367) combining observational analysis with two-sample Mendelian Randomization to examine the OSA-metabolic syndrome relationship. Found OSA associated with MetS OR 2.26 (1.82-2.80), abdominal obesity OR 4.76 (2.77-8.19), hyperglycemia OR 1.74, and hypertriglyceridemia OR 1.42. The MR analysis supports a causal bidirectional OSA-MetS relationship, complementing the target study's cross-sectional METS-IR findings.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Association between metabolic score for visceral fat and obstructive sleep apnea: a cross-sectional study
- **Authors**: Xu X, Xu J, Zhang M
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Medicine
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.3389/fmed.2024.1480717
- **PMID**: 39726679
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2013-2020 study examining a related metabolic index (METS-VF, metabolic score for visceral fat) and its association with OSA. Demonstrated a significant gender interaction (P < 0.05) in the METS-VF-OSA association, with stronger effects in females. Complements the target study by using a different metabolic index and more recent NHANES cycles while identifying similar sex-differential patterns.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Association of obstructive sleep apnea with cardiometabolic diseases and cardiovascular mortality
- **Authors**: Gao J, Shi L, Zhu X, Liu J
- **Journal**: The Clinical Respiratory Journal
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1111/crj.13666
- **PMID**: 37482921
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2005-2008 study (n=9,076) examining OSA associations with multiple cardiometabolic diseases and mortality. Found OSA positively associated with hypertension OR 1.28 (1.14-1.45) and diabetes OR 1.46 (1.22-1.76) but no significant association with all-cause or CVD mortality. Provides context for the clinical consequences of the metabolic-OSA relationship examined by the target study.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Obstructive Sleep Apnea Risk Is Associated With Severity of Metabolic Syndrome: A Secondary Analysis of the 2015-2018 National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey
- **Authors**: Kariuki JK, Yang K, Scott PW, Chasens ER, Godzik C, Luyster FS, Imes CC
- **Journal**: Journal of Cardiovascular Nursing
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1097/JCN.0000000000000868
- **PMID**: 34935745
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2015-2018 study using the Multivariable Apnea Prediction (MAP) index to assess OSA risk and its association with metabolic syndrome severity. Demonstrated that higher OSA risk is associated with more severe metabolic impairment. Subsequent work by the same group (Morris et al. 2023) showed the MAP index has differential sensitivity by sex (87.5% in men vs. 40% in women), highlighting the sex-dependent nature of OSA screening tool performance.
