# Related Work: Depression Prevalence Trends in the United States

## Reference 1
- **Title**: The trend and pattern of depression prevalence in the U.S.: Data from NHANES 2005 to 2016
- **Authors**: Sohrab Iranpour, Siamak Sabour, Fatemeh Koohi, Hossein Mozafar Saadati
- **Journal**: Journal of Affective Disorders
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jad.2021.11.027
- **PMID**: 34785265
- **Relevance**: Uses the same NHANES 2005-2016 data (n=31,191) to examine depression prevalence trends. Reports overall prevalence of 7.5% with significant increasing trends, particularly among females (approximately 2-fold increase). Provides complementary subgroup analyses showing steeper trends among older adults, white individuals, and higher-income groups.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Influence of the COVID-19 pandemic on the prevalence of depression in U.S. adults: evidence from NHANES
- **Authors**: Yun Jiang, Wusheng Deng, Mei Zhao
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-025-87593-5
- **PMID**: 39856194
- **Relevance**: Extends NHANES depression trend analysis through August 2023 (n=36,472), providing the most current temporal perspective. Shows depression prevalence jumped from 8.54% (pre-pandemic) to 12.39% (during pandemic), OR = 1.58. Contextualizes the 2005-2016 trends as part of a longer trajectory that accelerated during COVID-19.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Trends in Depression Prevalence in the USA from 2005 to 2015: Widening Disparities in Vulnerable Groups
- **Authors**: Weinberger AH, Gbedemah M, Martinez AM, Nash D, Galea S, Goodwin RD
- **Journal**: Psychological Medicine
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.1017/S0033291717002781
- **PMID**: 29021005
- **Relevance**: Landmark NHANES trend study (2005-2015) documenting widening depression disparities. Blue-collar workers showed steeper increases than white-collar, and disparities by income and education widened over time. Provides the health equity dimension that complements the age/severity-focused findings of Yu et al.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Depressive Symptoms Among US Adults During the Great Recession and Economic Recovery
- **Authors**: Annie Yu-An Chen, Roland Sturm
- **Journal**: Journal of Mental Health Policy and Economics
- **Year**: 2022
- **PMID**: 35302049
- **PMC**: PMC8944938
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2005-2018 analysis (n=31,389) examining how economic cycles influenced depression. PHQ-9 scores increased during the Great Recession (2007-2008) and remained elevated through 2017-2018 despite economic recovery. Provides macroeconomic context for interpreting temporal depression trends.
