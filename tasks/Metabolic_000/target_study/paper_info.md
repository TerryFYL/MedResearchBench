# Target Study: HOMA-IR for Metabolic Syndrome Prediction

## Metadata
- **Title**: Prediction of Metabolic Syndrome in U.S. Adults Using Homeostasis Model Assessment-Insulin Resistance
- **Authors**: Joel Guess, Thomas H. Beltran, Y. Sammy Choi
- **Journal**: Metabolic Syndrome and Related Disorders
- **Year**: 2023
- **Volume/Issue/Pages**: 21(3):156-162
- **DOI**: 10.1089/met.2022.0097
- **PMID**: 36787450

## Affiliation
Internal Medicine Service, Department of Medicine, and Department of Clinical Investigation, Womack Army Medical Center, Fort Bragg, North Carolina, USA

## Abstract Summary
This study assessed the efficacy of using insulin resistance (IR) estimated by the homeostasis model assessment (HOMA) as a predictor of metabolic syndrome (MetS) among U.S. adults. A serial, cross-sectional analysis of nationally representative data from NHANES between 2011 and 2018 was performed. Data from 8,897 nonpregnant adult participants representing 222 million individuals were analyzed. The estimated prevalence of MetS was 31.7% (n=2,958; 95% CI: 30.1-33.3). The optimal HOMA-IR to discriminate between individuals with and without MetS in the general population was 2.83 (sensitivity = 73.8%; specificity = 73.8%; area under the curve = 0.82). The authors concluded that HOMA-IR is a sensitive and specific method of screening for individuals with MetS.

## Key Methods
- Serial cross-sectional analysis of NHANES 2011-2018 (4 cycles)
- HOMA-IR calculated from fasting glucose and insulin
- MetS defined by harmonized criteria
- ROC curve analysis for optimal HOMA-IR cutpoint determination
- Survey-weighted analysis accounting for complex survey design
- Diagnostic performance evaluation (sensitivity, specificity, AUC)

## Key Findings
1. MetS prevalence among U.S. adults: 31.7% (95% CI: 30.1-33.3)
2. Optimal HOMA-IR cutoff for MetS: 2.83
3. Sensitivity: 73.8%
4. Specificity: 73.8%
5. AUC: 0.82 (indicating good discriminative ability)
6. HOMA-IR demonstrated as a practical screening tool for MetS

## Dataset Used
NHANES 2011-2012, 2013-2014, 2015-2016, 2017-2018 cycles (fasting subsample)
