# Related Work: HOMA-IR and Metabolic Syndrome Prediction

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Superiority of the triglyceride glucose index over the homeostasis model in predicting metabolic syndrome based on NHANES data analysis
- **Authors**: Wan H, Cao H, Ning P
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-024-66692-9
- **PMID**: 38969755
- **Relevance**: Largest NHANES-based comparison of HOMA-IR vs. TyG index for MetS prediction (n=34,195, NHANES 1999-2020). Found TyG slightly outperformed HOMA-IR (AUC 0.827 vs 0.784), establishing context for HOMA-IR's predictive performance and the emerging role of alternative insulin resistance indices.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Insulin Resistance and Cardiometabolic Risk Profile Among Nondiabetic American Young Adults: Insights From NHANES
- **Authors**: Parcha V, Heindl B, Kalra R, et al.
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1210/clinem/dgab645
- **PMID**: 34473288
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2007-2018 study of insulin resistance prevalence among young adults (18-44 years, n=6,247). Used HOMA-IR ≥2.5 cutoff; found 40-45% IR prevalence, with nearly half of IR individuals being nonobese. Highlights the epidemiologic burden and the importance of BMI-independent screening.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Insulin resistance (HOMA-IR) cut-off values and the metabolic syndrome in a general adult population: effect of gender and age (EPIRCE cross-sectional study)
- **Authors**: Esteghamati A, Ashraf H, Khalilzadeh O, et al.
- **Journal**: BMC Endocrine Disorders
- **Year**: 2013
- **DOI**: 10.1186/1472-6823-13-47
- **PMID**: 24131857
- **Relevance**: Landmark study using ROC regression methodology to evaluate age and gender effects on optimal HOMA-IR cutoffs for MetS. Demonstrated that cutoffs vary significantly by sex (1.85 in men vs. 2.07 in women) and that ROC regression with covariate adjustment provides more robust threshold estimation than fixed-percentile approaches.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Homeostasis model assessment of insulin resistance (HOMA-IR) and metabolic syndrome at baseline of a multicentric Brazilian cohort: ELSA-Brasil study
- **Authors**: Martins Neto V, et al.
- **Journal**: Cadernos de Saúde Pública
- **Year**: 2020
- **DOI**: 10.1590/0102-311X00072120
- **PMID**: 32901702
- **Relevance**: Large cohort study (n=12,313) demonstrating BMI-stratified HOMA-IR cutoffs for MetS — optimal cutoff increased with BMI category (lower in normal-weight, higher in obese), providing methodological precedent for BMI-stratified ROC analysis.
