# Related Work: Undiagnosed COPD Prevalence and Risk Factors

## Reference 1
- **Title**: The prevalence and mortality risks of PRISm and COPD in the United States from NHANES 2007-2012
- **Authors**: Cadham CJ, Oh H, Han MLK, Mannino DM, Cook S, Meza R, Levy DT, Sanchez-Romero LM
- **Journal**: Respiratory Research
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12931-024-02841-y
- **PMID**: 38750492
- **Relevance**: Most recent comprehensive NHANES 2007-2012 analysis of COPD prevalence by GOLD stages and mortality risks. Reports spirometry-defined COPD prevalence of 13.1-14.3% — far exceeding self-reported rates (~5%), quantifying the magnitude of the underdiagnosis gap. Provides mortality hazard ratios by GOLD stage linked to National Death Index.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Prevalence and factors associated with self-reported chronic obstructive pulmonary disease among adults aged 40-79: the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) 2007-2012
- **Authors**: Wheaton AG, Cunningham TJ, Ford ES, Croft JB
- **Journal**: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Diseases
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.15326/jcopdf.5.4.2017.0167
- **PMID**: 30294723
- **Relevance**: CDC-led analysis of factors associated with receiving a COPD diagnosis using the same NHANES 2007-2012 data. Found only 5.2% self-reported diagnosed COPD; identified respiratory symptoms (OR: 22.07 for 3+ symptoms), severe obstruction (OR: 4.90), and more healthcare visits (OR: 5.06) as strongest predictors of receiving a diagnosis — implying mild, asymptomatic cases are systematically missed.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Race and Gender Disparities are Evident in COPD Underdiagnoses Across all Severities of Measured Airflow Obstruction
- **Authors**: Mamary AJ, Stewart JI, Kinney GL, et al. (COPDGene Investigators)
- **Journal**: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Diseases
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.15326/jcopdf.5.3.2017.0145
- **PMID**: 30584581
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates race and gender disparities in COPD underdiagnosis using COPDGene data. African Americans less likely to be previously diagnosed at every severity level of measured obstruction. Provides complementary evidence from a smoking cohort supporting the NHANES findings of racial disparities in diagnostic patterns.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Underdiagnosis and Overdiagnosis of Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease
- **Authors**: Diab N, Gershon AS, Sin DD, Tan WC, Bourbeau J, Boulet LP, Aaron SD
- **Journal**: American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.1164/rccm.201804-0621CI
- **PMID**: 29979608
- **Relevance**: Comprehensive state-of-the-art review of COPD diagnostic accuracy globally. Reports approximately 70% worldwide underdiagnosis rate. Discusses the dual problem of underdiagnosis in the community and overdiagnosis in clinical settings, with 25-50% of COPD clinical diagnoses not confirmed by spirometry.
