# Related Work: Dietary Sodium Intake and Hypertension

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Association Between Urinary Sodium and Potassium Excretion and Blood Pressure Among Adults in the United States: National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey, 2014
- **Authors**: Jackson SL, Cogswell ME, Zhao L, et al.
- **Journal**: Circulation
- **Year**: 2018
- **DOI**: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.117.029193
- **PMID**: 29021321
- **Relevance**: Landmark NHANES study using gold-standard 24-hour urinary sodium excretion; found strong linear dose-response association between urinary sodium and blood pressure (4.58 mmHg SBP per 1000 mg sodium). Uses same NHANES framework but with biomarker-based sodium assessment.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: The role of body mass index in the association between dietary sodium intake and blood pressure: A mediation analysis with NHANES
- **Authors**: Feng S, Zhu Z, et al.
- **Journal**: Nutrition, Metabolism and Cardiovascular Diseases
- **Year**: 2021
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.numecd.2021.09.026
- **PMID**: 34629246
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2007-2016 study (n=12,262) demonstrating that BMI mediates the sodium-BP relationship; dietary sodium has both direct and indirect (via BMI) effects on systolic blood pressure. Provides context for why simple adjustment for BMI may attenuate the sodium-BP association.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Blood Pressure Effects of Sodium Reduction: Dose-Response Meta-Analysis of Experimental Studies
- **Authors**: Filippini T, Malavolti M, Whelton PK, Naska A, Orsini N, Vinceti M
- **Journal**: Circulation
- **Year**: 2021
- **DOI**: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.120.050371
- **PMID**: 33586450
- **Relevance**: Comprehensive dose-response meta-analysis of 85 RCTs confirming approximately linear relationship between sodium intake and BP reduction across the full range of dietary sodium exposure. Provides experimental evidence contrasting with null observational findings.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Effect of Dietary Sodium on Blood Pressure: A Crossover Trial
- **Authors**: Gupta DK, Lewis CE, Varady KA, et al.
- **Journal**: JAMA
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1001/jama.2023.23651
- **PMID**: 37950918
- **Relevance**: JAMA crossover trial (n=213) showing that low-sodium diet reduced systolic BP by ~8 mmHg vs high-sodium diet in 73.4% of participants regardless of hypertension status. Demonstrates individual-level sodium-BP response in a controlled setting.
