## Abstract Summary
This cross-sectional analysis used pooled NHANES 2007-2012 data (n=5,988 adults aged 20+ years) to examine associations between blood concentrations of 41 volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and airflow obstruction. VOCs were measured by headspace solid-phase microextraction/gas chromatography/isotope dilution mass spectrometry (SPME-GC-IDMS). Airflow obstruction was classified using pre- and post-bronchodilator spirometry as non-reversible (consistent with COPD per GOLD criteria) or reversible (consistent with asthma). Survey-weighted multivariable logistic regression adjusted for demographics, smoking pack-years, ETS exposure, BMI, and physical activity. Blood xylenes (m-/p-xylene OR: 1.18, o-xylene OR: 1.19), bromodichloromethane (OR: 1.23), and dibromochloromethane (OR: 1.27) were significantly associated with non-reversible airflow obstruction. Blood styrene (OR: 1.51) was associated with reversible airflow obstruction. This was the first study to identify VOCs differentially associated with non-reversible versus reversible obstruction, and the first to report trihalomethane (chlorinated water byproduct) associations with airflow obstruction in a general population.

## Key Methods
- Three pooled NHANES cycles (2007-2008, 2009-2010, 2011-2012) with 6-year combined weights
- Blood VOC measurement by headspace SPME-GC-IDMS (CDC Division of Laboratory Sciences)
- 41 blood VOCs screened; those with >= 10% detection included in analysis
- Airflow obstruction: pre-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC < 0.70
  - Non-reversible: post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC < 0.70 (COPD-like)
  - Reversible: post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC >= 0.70 (asthma-like)
- Survey-weighted multivariable logistic regression
- Covariates: age, poverty income ratio, BMI, pack-years (log), sex, race/ethnicity, current/past smoking, ETS exposure, physical activity
- Effect modification testing by smoking status (interaction terms)
- Sensitivity analyses excluding current smokers
- Complex survey design accounted for (NHANES weights, PSU, strata)

## Key Findings
1. Blood xylenes associated with non-reversible obstruction: m-/p-xylene (OR: 1.18, 95% CI: 1.04-1.33), o-xylene (OR: 1.19, 95% CI: 1.02-1.39)
2. Trihalomethanes associated with non-reversible obstruction: bromodichloromethane (OR: 1.23, 95% CI: 1.04-1.45), dibromochloromethane (OR: 1.27, 95% CI: 1.06-1.51)
3. Blood styrene associated with reversible obstruction (OR: 1.51, 95% CI: 1.02-2.23)
4. First study to differentiate VOC associations by obstruction reversibility (non-reversible = COPD-like vs reversible = asthma-like)
5. First population-based evidence linking trihalomethanes (chlorinated water disinfection byproducts) to airflow obstruction
6. First report of styrene-respiratory association at non-occupational exposure levels in a general population

## Dataset Used
NHANES 2007-2012 (3 combined cycles): spirometry + blood VOC subsample
