# Related Work: Environmental Pollutant Exposure and Lung Function

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Association of volatile organic compound levels with chronic obstructive pulmonary diseases in NHANES 2013-2016
- **Authors**: Li Y, Li H, et al.
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-024-67210-7
- **Relevance**: Uses NHANES 2013-2016 blood VOC levels (rather than urinary metabolites) to assess COPD prevalence associations. Employs restricted cubic splines, weighted quantile sum regression, and dual-pollution models. Extends VOC-respiratory disease link beyond spirometric outcomes to clinical COPD diagnosis.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Associations between environmental heavy metals exposure and preserved ratio impaired spirometry in the U.S. adults
- **Authors**: Zeng X, Xu X, et al.
- **Journal**: Environmental Science and Pollution Research
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s11356-023-29688-y
- **PMID**: 37733198
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2007-2012 study (n=9,556) linking serum cadmium, lead, and mercury to preserved ratio impaired spirometry (PRISm) and lung function. Serum cadmium strongly associated with PRISm risk (OR=2.517 in never/former smokers). Demonstrates environmental pollutant-lung function paradigm using NHANES spirometry data.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Diminished lung function mediating the associations between blood levels of volatile organic compounds and respiratory morbidity and mortality
- **Authors**: Fan Y, Wang Y, et al.
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-025-23017-8
- **Relevance**: NHANES 2007-2012 analysis (n=6,156) using Bayesian Kernel Machine Regression (BKMR) to model blood VOC mixture effects on lung function, then mediation analysis linking VOC-induced lung function decline to asthma, emphysema, and chronic bronchitis outcomes. Extends beyond exposure-outcome to causal pathway elucidation.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: The Correlation between Metal Mixed Exposure and Lung Function in Different Ages of the Population
- **Authors**: Huang H, Guan X, et al.
- **Journal**: Toxics
- **Year**: 2024
- **PMID**: 38535810
- **Relevance**: NHANES-based analysis examining heavy metal mixture effects on spirometry across age groups. Found cadmium and lead consistently negatively correlated with FEV1, FVC, FEF25-75%, and PEF across children, adults, and elderly populations. Provides age-stratified context for environmental pollutant-lung function research.
