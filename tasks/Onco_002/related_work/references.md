# Related Work: Socioeconomic Status and Late-Stage Cancer Diagnosis

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Impact of socioeconomic status on cancer incidence and stage at diagnosis: selected findings from the surveillance, epidemiology, and end results: National Longitudinal Mortality Study
- **Authors**: Clegg LX, Reichman ME, Miller BA, et al.
- **Journal**: Cancer Causes & Control
- **Year**: 2009
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s10552-008-9256-0
- **PMID**: 19002764
- **Relevance**: Foundational SEER-NLMS study demonstrating that lower individual-level SES is consistently associated with later-stage cancer diagnosis across multiple cancer types. Uses individual-level SES data linked to SEER, providing the methodological foundation for area-level SES analyses.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Enduring Cancer Disparities by Persistent Poverty, Rurality, and Race: 1990-1992 to 2014-2018
- **Authors**: Moss JL, Pinto CN, Srinivasan S, Cronin KA, Croyle RT
- **Journal**: Journal of the National Cancer Institute (JNCI)
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1093/jnci/djac038
- **PMID**: 35238347
- **Relevance**: Landmark SEER-based study showing that cancer mortality disparities by persistent poverty, rurality, and race have endured over nearly three decades (1990-2018). Demonstrates that persistent poverty counties have higher mortality across multiple cancer types. Published in JNCI, the NCI's flagship journal.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Description of census-tract-level social determinants of health in cancer surveillance data
- **Authors**: Bhattacharya M, Cronin KA, Farrigan TL, Kennedy AE, Yu M, Srinivasan S
- **Journal**: JNCI Monographs
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1093/jncimonographs/lgae027
- **PMID**: 39102885
- **Relevance**: NCI-authored paper introducing the SEER specialized census-tract-level dataset with Yost Index (SES) and persistent poverty measures. Demonstrates that persistent-poverty tracts have higher percentages of later-stage diagnoses and lower 5-year survival across 8 cancer sites. Provides the methodological framework for modern SEER-based SES disparity analyses.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Neighborhood level socioeconomic disparities are associated with reduced colorectal cancer survival
- **Authors**: Loomans-Kropp HA, Elsaid MI, Kweon Y, et al.
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-025-17659-x
- **PMID**: 40998940
- **Relevance**: Recent SEER analysis (2006-2020, N=253,141) using overlap propensity score weighting with marginal structural models to estimate mortality risk by persistent poverty and Yost Index SES quintile. Found that persistent poverty residence was associated with ARD of 7.2 for all-cause mortality at 15-year follow-up. Uses modern causal inference methods to quantify the SES-survival association.
