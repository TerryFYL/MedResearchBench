# Related Work: Colorectal Cancer Stage-Specific Survival Trends

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Oncologic outcomes and trends in each colon cancer location and stages over the last two decades: insights from the SEER registry
- **Authors**: Not fully listed (multi-author study)
- **Journal**: Techniques in Coloproctology
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s10151-024-03020-3
- **Relevance**: Comprehensive SEER analysis (2000-2019) evaluating survival disparities by anatomic colon segment and stage over two decades. Shows notable improvement in overall and cancer-specific survival across all colon segments and stages in patients who underwent curative treatment. Complements the target study by adding tumor location as an additional dimension.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Colorectal cancer statistics, 2023
- **Authors**: Siegel RL, Wagle NS, Cercek A, Smith RA, Jemal A
- **Journal**: CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.3322/caac.21772
- **Relevance**: Authoritative biennial review of CRC epidemiology including incidence, mortality, and survival trends. Reports that age-adjusted CRC mortality rates declined 1.3% per year (2014-2023) and highlights the rising incidence in adults younger than 50. Provides the epidemiological context for stage-specific survival analysis.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Survival outcome and prognostic factors for early-onset and late-onset metastatic colorectal cancer: a population based study from SEER database
- **Authors**: Multi-author (Nature Scientific Reports)
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-024-54972-3
- **Relevance**: SEER-based study (2010-2019) comparing survival outcomes between early-onset (<50) and late-onset metastatic CRC. Highlights the rising incidence of early-onset CRC and identifies distinct prognostic factors by age group. Relevant as a complementary analysis focusing on metastatic disease and age-specific trends.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Racial and socioeconomic disparities in survival improvement of eight cancers
- **Authors**: Shaw V, Zhang B, Tang M, et al.
- **Journal**: BJC Reports
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s44276-024-00044-y
- **Relevance**: SEER-based cohort study (2004-2018, N=1,875,281) of eight common cancers showing that while cancer-free survival improved overall, Black patient CSS from 2014-2018 remained lower than White patient CSS from 2004-2008 for breast and prostate cancer. Provides broader context for understanding how CRC disparities compare to other cancer types.
