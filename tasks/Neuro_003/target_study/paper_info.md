# Target Study: Dietary Antioxidant Index and Parkinson's Disease Prevalence and Mortality

## Metadata

## Abstract Summary
This study investigated the relationship between the Composite Dietary Antioxidant Index (CDAI) and both Parkinson's disease (PD) prevalence and all-cause mortality in PD patients using NHANES 2007-2018 data (n=34,133 adults). CDAI was calculated from six dietary antioxidants (vitamins A, C, E, carotenoids, selenium, zinc). PD was identified by prescription of PD-specific medications (510 PD cases, 33,623 controls). Survey-weighted logistic regression showed that participants in the middle (Q2) and highest (Q3) CDAI tertiles had approximately 30% lower odds of PD compared to the lowest tertile (Q1). Among PD patients, survey-weighted Cox regression linked to the National Death Index demonstrated that CDAI below 0.527 significantly increased risk of all-cause mortality. Restricted cubic spline analysis revealed a nonlinear relationship between CDAI and PD prevalence with a turning point at CDAI = 0.471. The protective effect of higher CDAI was most pronounced in individuals over age 60, smokers, and those with comorbid hypertension.

## Key Methods
- Six pooled NHANES cycles (2007-2008 through 2017-2018) with combined weights
- CDAI calculation: sum of standardized dietary intakes of vitamins A, C, E, carotenoids, selenium, zinc (from 24-hour recalls + supplements)
- PD identification: prescription of carbidopa, levodopa, entacapone, amantadine, pramipexole, or bromocriptine
- CDAI tertiles: Q1 (<-1.07), Q2 (-1.07 to 1.74), Q3 (>1.74)
- Survey-weighted logistic regression (PD prevalence)
- Survey-weighted Cox proportional hazards regression (all-cause mortality via NDI linkage)
- Restricted cubic spline regression (dose-response nonlinearity)
- Three progressive adjustment models (unadjusted; age/gender/hypertension/diabetes; + fasting glucose, ALT, BMI, triglycerides, total cholesterol)
- Subgroup analyses by age, gender, smoking, hypertension, diabetes, alcohol use

## Key Findings
1. PD prevalence — Q2 vs Q1: OR 0.70 (95% CI: 0.51-0.96); Q3 vs Q1: OR 0.72 (95% CI: 0.54-0.98) in fully adjusted model
2. PD mortality — Q2 vs Q1: HR 0.56 (95% CI: 0.35-0.89); Q3 vs Q1: HR 0.53 (95% CI: 0.31-0.90) in fully adjusted model
3. Nonlinear dose-response: CDAI turning point at 0.471 for PD prevalence (RCS analysis)
4. Mortality threshold: CDAI < 0.527 significantly increases all-cause mortality risk in PD patients
5. Subgroup: protective effect most pronounced in age >60, smokers, and hypertensive individuals
6. First study to examine CDAI-PD association and CDAI-mortality relationship in PD patients using NHANES

## Dataset Used
NHANES 2007-2018 (6 combined cycles) with National Death Index linked mortality follow-up
