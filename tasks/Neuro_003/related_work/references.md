# Related Work: Dietary Antioxidants and Parkinson's Disease

## References

### 1. Liu et al. (2023) — Dietary intake and risk of Parkinson's disease (NHANES 2007-2016)
- **Title**: Association between dietary intake and risk of Parkinson's disease: cross-sectional analysis of survey data from NHANES 2007-2016
- **Authors**: Liu L, Shen Q, Bao Y, Xu F, Zhang D, Huang H, Tu L, Xu Y
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Nutrition, 2023; 10:1278128
- **DOI**: 10.3389/fnut.2023.1278128
- **PMID**: 38192644
- **Relevance**: Uses overlapping NHANES cycles (2007-2016) and examines individual nutrient intakes including zinc, vitamin C, vitamin K and their association with PD risk. Found dietary zinc and vitamin C protective against PD. Complementary to CDAI approach by examining individual nutrients rather than composite index.

### 2. Hao et al. (2024) — Dietary vitamin E intake and Parkinson's disease risk (NHANES 2009-2018)
- **Title**: Dietary vitamin E intake and risk of Parkinson's disease: a cross-sectional study
- **Authors**: Hao X, Li H, Li Q, Gao D, Wang X, Wu C, Wang Q, Zhu M
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Nutrition, 2024; 10:1289238
- **DOI**: 10.3389/fnut.2023.1289238
- **PMID**: 38249609
- **Relevance**: Focuses specifically on vitamin E — the antioxidant with strongest evidence for PD protection — using NHANES 2009-2018 (n=13,340). Employs propensity score matching plus weighted logistic regression. Found inverse association between vitamin E intake and PD risk, supporting the CDAI composite approach.

### 3. Talebi et al. (2022) — Systematic review and dose-response meta-analysis of dietary antioxidants and PD risk
- **Title**: Dietary Antioxidants and Risk of Parkinson's Disease: A Systematic Review and Dose-Response Meta-analysis of Observational Studies
- **Authors**: Talebi S, Ghoreishy SM, Jayedi A, Travica N, Mohammadi H
- **Journal**: Advances in Nutrition, 2022; 13(5):1493-1504
- **DOI**: 10.1093/advances/nmac001
- **PMID**: 35030236
- **Relevance**: Comprehensive meta-analysis of 448,737 participants with 4,654 PD cases. Found pooled RR for highest vs lowest vitamin E intake = 0.84 (95% CI: 0.71-0.99) and anthocyanins = 0.76 (95% CI: 0.61-0.96). Provides meta-analytic context for interpreting NHANES-based single-study findings.

### 4. Niu et al. (2023) — Vitamin C, E, beta-carotene and PD risk meta-analysis
- **Title**: Vitamin C, vitamin E, beta-carotene and risk of Parkinson's disease: a systematic review and dose-response meta-analysis of observational studies
- **Authors**: Niu F, Xie W, Zhang W, Kawuki J, Yu X
- **Journal**: Nutritional Neuroscience, 2024; 27(4):329-341
- **DOI**: 10.1080/1028415X.2023.2192550
- **PMID**: 36961747
- **Relevance**: Dose-response meta-analysis examining three specific antioxidant vitamins and PD risk. Concluded vitamin E intake significantly reduces PD risk while vitamin C shows weaker evidence. Supports the composite CDAI approach where vitamin E contributes substantially to the overall protective effect.
