## Abstract Summary
Using NHANES 2013-2018 data (n=16,531 non-pregnant U.S. adults aged >= 18 years), this study examined the hypertension control cascade (prevalence, awareness, treatment, and control) stratified by race/ethnicity (White, Black, Hispanic, Asian, Other). Among approximately 76.9 million U.S. adults with hypertension, 48.6% had controlled blood pressure. Compared with White adults (49.0% control), BP control rates were significantly lower in Black (39.2%; aOR 0.71, 95% CI 0.59-0.85), Hispanic (40.0%; aOR 0.71, 95% CI 0.58-0.88), and Asian (37.8%; aOR 0.68, 95% CI 0.55-0.84) Americans. Black individuals had the highest hypertension prevalence (45.3%). Treatment rates were similar for White and Black adults (~67%), but lower for Hispanic (60.5%) and Asian (58.8%) adults. The study revealed persistent disparities in the hypertension control cascade across racial/ethnic groups.

## Key Methods
- Pooled NHANES 2013-2014, 2015-2016, 2017-2018 (3 cycles)
- Survey-weighted multivariable logistic regression
- Age-standardization using direct method
- Hypertension control cascade: prevalence -> awareness -> treatment -> control
- Adjusted for age, sex, insurance, education, income, BMI, diabetes, CVD history

## Key Findings
1. Black adults: highest prevalence (45.3%), lowest control rate (39.2%)
2. Asian adults: lowest control rate (37.8%) despite relatively lower prevalence
3. Hispanic adults: lower awareness (71.5%) and treatment (60.5%) rates
4. White adults: highest control rate (49.0%) as reference group
5. Treatment intensification gap: similar treatment rates but lower control in Black adults suggests treatment quality/intensification issues
6. Insurance and SES partially but incompletely explain disparities

## Dataset Used
NHANES 2013-2014, 2015-2016, 2017-2018 (pooled)
