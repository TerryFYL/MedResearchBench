# Related Work: Hypertension Racial/Ethnic Disparities

## Reference 1
- **Title**: National Trends in Racial and Ethnic Disparities in Antihypertensive Medication Use and Blood Pressure Control Among Adults With Hypertension, 2011-2018
- **Authors**: Commodore-Mensah Y, Selvin E, Aber J, et al.
- **Journal**: Hypertension
- **Year**: 2021
- **DOI**: 10.1161/HYPERTENSIONAHA.121.18381
- **Relevance**: Complementary NHANES analysis examining trends in racial/ethnic disparities in antihypertensive medication use and BP control. Found Black adults had similar awareness and more intensive medication but lower control rates, suggesting downstream factors beyond access.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Racial and Ethnic Disparities in Hypertension: Barriers and Opportunities to Improve Blood Pressure Control
- **Authors**: Abrahamowicz AA, Ebinger J, Whelton SP, Commodore-Mensah Y, Yang E
- **Journal**: Current Cardiology Reports
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s11886-022-01826-x
- **PMID**: 36622491
- **Relevance**: Comprehensive review characterizing barriers and opportunities for reducing racial/ethnic disparities in BP control. Identifies social determinants of health (healthcare access, SES, food access, health literacy) as major drivers.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Trends in Blood Pressure Control among US Adults With Hypertension, 2013-2014 to 2021-2023
- **Authors**: Muntner P, Hardy ST, Fine LJ, et al.
- **Journal**: American Journal of Hypertension
- **Year**: 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1093/ajh/hpae152
- **Relevance**: Most recent NHANES-based analysis extending through 2021-2023 cycle. Found BP control did not improve from 2017-2020 to 2021-2023 overall, but Non-Hispanic Black adults showed some increase in control during this period.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Social Determinants of Health and Disparities in Hypertension and Cardiovascular Diseases
- **Authors**: Chaturvedi A, Zhu J, Engel RJ, Patel VN
- **Journal**: Hypertension
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1161/HYPERTENSIONAHA.123.21973
- **PMID**: 38152897
- **Relevance**: Review examining social determinants (structural racism, neighborhood factors, economic stability) driving hypertension and CVD disparities. Provides framework for understanding why racial/ethnic BP control gaps persist despite clinical interventions.
