# Related Work: Blood Heavy Metals and Cognitive Function

## References

### 1. Song et al. (2023) — Sex-specific mixture effects of blood heavy metals on cognitive function
- **Title**: Sex Specificity in the Mixed Effects of Blood Heavy Metals and Cognitive Function on Elderly: Evidence from NHANES
- **Authors**: Song Y, Liu Y, Liu X, et al.
- **Journal**: Nutrients, 2023; 15(13):2874
- **DOI**: 10.3390/nu15132874
- **PMID**: 37447200
- **Relevance**: Uses same NHANES 2011-2014 data and cognitive tests (AFT, DSST, CERAD) but employs advanced mixture methods (BKMR, WQS, quantile g-computation) rather than traditional logistic regression. Found blood cadmium and lead negatively associated with cognition, selenium protective, with significant sex differences. Complementary methodological approach to Lu et al.

### 2. Tang, Shen & Hong (2024) — Trace elements, cognitive function, and dietary inflammation
- **Title**: Uncovering the relationship between trace element exposure, cognitive function, and dietary inflammation index in elderly Americans from the NHANES 2011-2014
- **Authors**: Tang J, Shen L, Hong L
- **Journal**: BMC Public Health, 2024; 24(1):2516
- **DOI**: 10.1186/s12889-024-20060-4
- **PMID**: 39285378
- **Relevance**: Examines same NHANES cycles and metals but adds dietary inflammation index as effect modifier. Found cadmium negatively associated with DSST (beta=-1.17), lead negatively associated with IRT, and that anti-inflammatory diet may attenuate metal neurotoxicity. Extends understanding of how diet modifies metal-cognition associations.

### 3. Fu, Xu, Cao et al. (2024) — Single and joint metal exposure and cognitive function
- **Title**: Single and joint exposure of Pb, Cd, Hg, Se, Cu, and Zn were associated with cognitive function of older adults
- **Authors**: Fu Y, Xu J, Cao X, et al.
- **Journal**: Scientific Reports, 2024; 14:28567
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41598-024-79720-5
- **Relevance**: Expands the metal panel to include copper and zinc alongside Pb, Cd, Hg, Se using NHANES 2011-2014. Applies quantile regression, restricted cubic splines (RCS), and BKMR to capture non-linear dose-response relationships. Found copper negatively associated with DSST, zinc positively associated with memory. Demonstrates importance of considering both essential and toxic metals jointly.

### 4. Laouali et al. (2022) — Blood metals mixtures and cognitive performance with dietary modification
- **Title**: Association between blood metals mixtures concentrations and cognitive performance, and effect modification by diet in older US adults
- **Authors**: Laouali N, Benmarhnia T, Lanphear BP, et al.
- **Journal**: Environmental Epidemiology, 2022; 6(1):e192
- **DOI**: 10.1097/EE9.0000000000000192
- **PMID**: 35169670
- **Relevance**: Pioneering application of quantile g-computation to metal mixture-cognition associations in NHANES 2011-2014 (n=1,777 adults 60+). Found that joint metal mixture exposure was negatively associated with DSST scores, and that dietary quality modified these associations. Provides methodological foundation for mixture-based approaches.
