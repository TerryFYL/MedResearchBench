# Target Study: TSH and Mortality in Euthyroid Diabetic Adults

## Metadata

## Abstract Summary
This study assessed whether thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) levels within the euthyroid range are associated with all-cause and cardiovascular mortality in individuals with diabetes using NHANES III (1988-1994) data linked to the National Death Index through December 31, 2019. Among 1,830 euthyroid adults with diabetes (median follow-up 17.1 years, 1,324 all-cause deaths, 525 CVD deaths), restricted cubic spline analysis revealed significant U-shaped associations between continuous TSH and both all-cause mortality (P for nonlinearity < 0.05) and cardiovascular mortality (P for nonlinearity < 0.05). Compared to the medium-normal TSH tertile (1.30-2.09 mIU/L), both low-normal TSH (HR 1.39, 95% CI 1.12-1.73) and high-normal TSH (HR 1.31, 95% CI 1.07-1.61) were associated with significantly elevated all-cause mortality. For CVD mortality, the pattern was similar: low-normal TSH HR 1.69 (1.17-2.44) and high-normal TSH HR 1.52 (1.08-2.12).

## Key Methods
- Prospective cohort study using NHANES III (1988-1994) with NDI mortality linkage through December 31, 2019
- Euthyroid defined as TSH 0.39-4.60 mIU/L with normal TT4
- Restricted cubic splines (RCS) with 3 knots at 25th, 50th, 75th percentiles for nonlinear dose-response modeling
- Multivariable Cox proportional hazards regression with three progressive adjustment models
- Subgroup analyses stratified by age (<60 vs ≥60 years) and sex
- Sensitivity analyses adjusting for urinary iodine, TPO-Ab, TT4, HEI, frailty, and CRP
- Complex survey design properly accounted for with NHANES III sampling weights

## Key Findings
1. U-shaped association between TSH and all-cause mortality (P for nonlinearity < 0.05)
2. U-shaped association between TSH and cardiovascular mortality (P for nonlinearity < 0.05)
3. Low-normal TSH: all-cause HR 1.39 (95% CI 1.12-1.73), CVD HR 1.69 (1.17-2.44)
4. High-normal TSH: all-cause HR 1.31 (95% CI 1.07-1.61), CVD HR 1.52 (1.08-2.12)
5. Medium-normal TSH (1.30-2.09 mIU/L) represents the nadir of mortality risk
6. Associations robust across sensitivity analyses adjusting for multiple potential confounders

## Dataset Used
NHANES III (1988-1994) with National Death Index (NDI) linked mortality follow-up through December 31, 2019 (median follow-up 17.1 years)
