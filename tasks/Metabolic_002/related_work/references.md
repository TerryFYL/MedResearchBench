# Related Work: Thyroid Function and Mortality

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Association Between Serum Thyrotropin Levels and Mortality Among Euthyroid Adults in the United States
- **Authors**: Inoue K, Tsujimoto T, Saito J, Sugiyama T
- **Journal**: Thyroid
- **Year**: 2016
- **DOI**: 10.1089/thy.2016.0156
- **PMID**: 27539006
- **Relevance**: Foundational NHANES III study of TSH and mortality in the general euthyroid population (n=12,584, median follow-up 19.1 years). Found that high-normal TSH tertile was associated with all-cause mortality HR 1.27 (1.10-1.47) and CVD mortality HR 1.30 (1.02-1.67) compared to medium-normal TSH. Used categorical TSH tertile analysis (not RCS), establishing the basic pattern that the target study later confirmed with formal nonlinear modeling in the diabetic subpopulation.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Trends in Prevalence of Thyroid Dysfunction and its Associations With Mortality Among US Participants, 1988-2012
- **Authors**: Zhang X, Wang Y, Wang H, Zhang X
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1210/clinem/dgad553
- **PMID**: 37738422
- **Relevance**: Multi-survey study combining NHANES III (1988-1994) with NHANES 1999-2002 and 2007-2012 (n=33,117) examining temporal trends in thyroid dysfunction prevalence and mortality associations. Found subclinical hypothyroidism associated with higher mortality among those aged ≥65 (HR 1.17, 95% CI 1.00-1.37). Provides broader population-level context for the TSH-mortality relationship across time periods.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Low Thyroid Function in Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease Is an Independent Predictor of All-Cause and Cardiovascular Mortality
- **Authors**: Kim D, Vazquez-Montesino LM, Li AA, Cholankeril G, Ahmed A
- **Journal**: American Journal of Gastroenterology
- **Year**: 2020
- **DOI**: 10.14309/ajg.0000000000000740
- **PMID**: 32496342
- **Relevance**: NHANES III study (n=9,085) demonstrating that low-normal thyroid function (TSH 2.5-4.5 mIU/L) is an independent predictor of mortality in NAFLD patients. Uses the same NHANES III-NDI linkage as the target study and provides disease-specific context for the TSH-mortality association in metabolic disease populations (NAFLD vs. diabetes).

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Subclinical thyroid dysfunction and the risk of heart failure events: an individual participant data analysis from 6 prospective cohorts
- **Authors**: Gencer B, Collet TH, Virgini V, et al. (Thyroid Studies Collaboration)
- **Journal**: Circulation
- **Year**: 2012
- **DOI**: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.112.096024
- **PMID**: 22869857
- **Relevance**: Landmark individual participant data meta-analysis of 25,390 participants from 6 prospective cohorts demonstrating that both subclinical hypothyroidism (TSH 10-19.9 mIU/L) and subclinical hyperthyroidism (TSH <0.10 mIU/L) increase heart failure risk. Provides the conceptual framework for why both ends of the TSH spectrum are associated with adverse cardiovascular outcomes, extending to the narrower within-euthyroid range examined in the target study.
