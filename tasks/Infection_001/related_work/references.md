# Related Work: HPV Vaccine Effectiveness Evaluation

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Prevalence of HPV After Introduction of the Vaccination Program in the United States
- **Authors**: Markowitz LE, Liu G, Hariri S, Steinau M, Dunne EF, Unger ER
- **Journal**: Pediatrics
- **Year**: 2016
- **DOI**: 10.1542/peds.2015-1968
- **PMID**: 26908697
- **Relevance**: Earlier NHANES-based HPV prevalence analysis (2003-2014) from the same CDC research group. Documented initial declines in 4vHPV-type prevalence among females 14-19 years (64% reduction) and emerging declines in ages 20-24. Serves as the methodological predecessor to Rosenblum et al. 2022, which extended the analysis through 2018 with additional focus on male data and herd effects.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Declines in Prevalence of Human Papillomavirus Vaccine-Type Infection Among Females after Introduction of Vaccine — United States, 2003-2018
- **Authors**: Rosenblum HG, Lewis RM, Gargano JW, Querec TD, Unger ER, Markowitz LE
- **Journal**: MMWR Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report
- **Year**: 2021
- **DOI**: 10.15585/mmwr.mm7012a2
- **PMID**: 33764964
- **Relevance**: Same authorship team and dataset (NHANES 2003-2018) but published as MMWR brief. Reports 88% 4vHPV prevalence reduction among females 14-19 and 81% among females 20-24 from prevaccine era. Includes the finding that prevalence declined by 87% even among unvaccinated females 14-19 (herd effect). The companion Annals paper (Rosenblum 2022) provides the full analytical framework.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Prevalence of Human Papillomavirus Infection Among Women Born in the 1990s vs the 1980s and Association With HPV Vaccination in the US
- **Authors**: Egemen D, Cheung LC, Engel A, et al.
- **Journal**: JAMA Network Open
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2022.34262
- **PMID**: 36200637
- **Relevance**: NHANES-based birth cohort analysis demonstrating that women vaccinated before sexual debut had near-zero HPV 16/18 prevalence, while those vaccinated after debut had intermediate protection. Published in JAMA Network Open (IF~13). Provides complementary evidence on vaccine timing that the Rosenblum et al. study did not specifically address.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: Long-Term Effectiveness of Human Papillomavirus Vaccination: Implications for Future Reduction in Cancer
- **Authors**: Markowitz LE, Schiller JT
- **Journal**: Annals of Internal Medicine
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.7326/M22-1309
- **PMID**: 36315938
- **Relevance**: Accompanying editorial in the same Annals issue as Rosenblum et al. 2022. Provides expert interpretation of the 12-year impact data and discusses implications for cancer prevention — estimates that current vaccination coverage could prevent ~90% of HPV-attributable cancers in the fully vaccinated cohort as they age.
