## Abstract Summary
This serial cross-sectional study used NHANES data from 2003-2018 to evaluate the population-level impact and individual-level effectiveness of quadrivalent HPV (4vHPV) vaccination among sexually experienced U.S. persons aged 14-24 years. Female cervicovaginal and male penile swab specimens were tested for HPV DNA. Among sexually experienced females aged 14-24 in 2015-2018, vaccine impact on 4vHPV-type prevalence was 85% overall, 90% among vaccinated females, and 74% among unvaccinated females (indicating strong herd protection). Vaccine effectiveness ranged from 60% to 84% during vaccine eras for females and was 51% during 2013-2016 for males. No significant declines were found in non-4vHPV-type prevalence, suggesting no type replacement. Female vaccination coverage among sexually experienced 14-24 year-olds increased from 25% (2007-2010) to 59% (2015-2018); male coverage increased from 27% (2011-2014) to 50% (2015-2018).

## Key Methods
- Survey-weighted HPV DNA prevalence estimation across 4 eras (prevaccine + 3 vaccine eras)
- Vaccine impact = percent reduction from prevaccine era prevalence (overall and by vaccination status)
- Vaccine effectiveness = 1 - prevalence ratio (vaccinated/unvaccinated) within each era
- Herd immunity quantification: prevalence reduction among unvaccinated persons
- Type replacement monitoring: non-4vHPV type prevalence trends
- Age-stratified analysis (14-19 and 20-24 years)
- Complex survey design accounted for (weights, PSU, strata)

## Key Findings
1. Overall 4vHPV-type impact in 2015-2018: 85% reduction from prevaccine era among females 14-24
2. Among vaccinated females: 90% impact (near-elimination of vaccine types)
3. Among unvaccinated females: 74% impact (strong herd protection)
4. Female vaccine effectiveness: 60-84% across vaccine eras
5. Male vaccine effectiveness: 51% in 2013-2016
6. No significant changes in non-4vHPV-type prevalence (no type replacement evidence)
7. Female vaccination coverage: 25% (2007-2010) → 59% (2015-2018)
8. Male vaccination coverage: 27% (2011-2014) → 50% (2015-2018)

## Dataset Used
NHANES 2003-2004 through 2017-2018 (8 consecutive 2-year cycles)
