# Figure Descriptions: Le et al. (2020) JAMA Network Open

## Figure 1: Trends in Hepatitis B Vaccination Coverage by Age Group (1999-2016)
Line plot showing the proportion of US-born children/adolescents with self-reported complete hepatitis B vaccination across nine NHANES cycles, stratified by age group (2-5, 6-10, 11-13, 14-18 years). All age groups show upward trends from ~60-65% in 1999 to ~85-90% in 2016.

## Figure 2: Trends in Serologic Evidence of Vaccine-Induced Immunity by Age Group (1999-2016)
Line plot showing the proportion with anti-HBs >= 10 mIU/mL (and anti-HBc negative) across the same cycles and age strata. Dramatic divergence by age: youngest (2-5) relatively stable, older groups show steep declines — most dramatic in ages 14-18 (from ~68.5% to ~15.6%).

## Figure 3: Vaccination Coverage vs. Serologic Immunity Comparison
Dual-panel or overlaid figure highlighting the paradoxical divergence between increasing vaccination coverage and declining serologic immunity, particularly in older adolescents. This is the key visual demonstrating the waning antibody phenomenon.

Note: Actual figures are from the published paper. See JAMA Network Open DOI: 10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.22388 for original figures.
