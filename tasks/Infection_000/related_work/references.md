# Related Work: Hepatitis B Seroprevalence and Vaccination Coverage Trends

## Reference 1
- **Title**: Prevalence and Trends in Hepatitis B Virus Infection in the United States, 2015-2018
- **Authors**: Roberts H, Ly KN, Yin S, Hughes E, Teshale E, Jiles R
- **Journal**: CDC NCHS Data Brief (No. 361)
- **Year**: 2020
- **PMID**: 32487291
- **Relevance**: CDC-authored NHANES-based data brief covering adult HBV seroprevalence and vaccination trends 1999-2018. Reports declining past/present HBV infection (5.7% to 4.3%) and increasing adult vaccination immunity (12.3% to 25.2%). Provides adult population context complementing Le et al.'s pediatric focus.

## Reference 2
- **Title**: Prevalence of chronic hepatitis B virus (HBV) infection in U.S. households: National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES), 1988-2012
- **Authors**: Roberts H, Kruszon-Moran D, Ly KN, Hughes E, Iqbal K, Jiles RB, Holmberg SD
- **Journal**: Hepatology
- **Year**: 2016
- **DOI**: 10.1002/hep.28109
- **PMID**: 26251317
- **Relevance**: Longest NHANES time series for HBV prevalence (1988-2012), documenting stable chronic HBV prevalence at 0.3% since 1999 despite 16% increase in vaccine-induced immunity. Provides the historical baseline against which Le et al.'s waning immunity findings should be interpreted.

## Reference 3
- **Title**: Disparities in Hepatitis B Vaccine Coverage by Race/Ethnicity: The National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) 2015-2016
- **Authors**: Hang Pham, Taft DH, Mouzaki M, et al.
- **Journal**: Vaccines
- **Year**: 2020
- **DOI**: 10.3390/vaccines8030372
- **PMID**: 32316174
- **Relevance**: Documents racial/ethnic disparities in adult HBV vaccine coverage using NHANES 2015-2016: overall prevalence only 23.3%, with Mexican Americans (OR 0.57) and African Americans (OR 0.70) having significantly lower coverage. Highlights equity dimension absent from Le et al.'s primarily pediatric analysis.

## Reference 4
- **Title**: The prevalence of hepatitis B virus infection in the United States in the era of vaccination
- **Authors**: Wasley A, Kruszon-Moran D, Kuhnert W, Simard EP, Finelli L, McQuillan G, Bell B
- **Journal**: Journal of Infectious Diseases
- **Year**: 2010
- **DOI**: 10.1086/651649
- **PMID**: 20533878
- **Relevance**: Foundational analysis comparing NHANES 1988-1994 with 1999-2006 to assess vaccination era impact on HBV epidemiology. Found no significant change in age-adjusted HBV infection prevalence despite vaccination rollout, establishing the paradox of stable infection rates amid increasing coverage that Le et al. further explored through the immunity waning lens.
